About Me

Howdy!

My name is Lalit Bauskar – don’t worry, I can’t pronounce it correctly either. I’m an undergraduate at Texas A&M University where I’m majoring in Computer Science and taking a minor in Mathematics. Currently, I’m looking for an internship for the summer of 2021.

Before I drank the maroon Kool-Aid, I grew up in Brownsville, Texas. I graduated from Homer Hanna Early College High School where I was voted “Least Likely to Get a Haircut”. Back then, I competed at the national level in robotics competitions; my time spent afterschool tinkering with our robots and rewriting their code was what got me interested in pursuing a Bachelor’s in Computer Science. Though I’ve loved puzzles, building stuff, and technology ever since I was a little kid.

I’ve gone to the barber a few times since high school, and my time at college has helped me appreciate things outside my major. My favorite part of attending Texas A&M is being able to meet so many wonderful people, each with their own unique background and perspective. I’ve become friends with people who, in the past, I never would have thought would even come close to entering my social circle, and I’m a better person for it. Being in the company of such a diverse group of people has broadened my perspective and introduced ideas and concepts that I would have never though of myself. I think it’s easy for programmers to get lost in a sea of algorithms, languages, and the bleeding edge of technology, and we often neglect to foster our soft skills as much as our left-brained ones. My role as a peer mentor last year and an RA this year has helped me put people ahead of technology, because they ultimately matter more. The best software in the world doesn’t mean anything if no one uses it.

That’s not to say I don’t spend a lot of time programming. I’ve worked on projects with the Aggie Coding Club for the past year, and I was a member of Project SOAR – where we wrote the firmware for a drone we designed and built. I’ve also made some personal projects which you can see on my GitHub or portfolio page. In the future, I hope to combine my experiences with software development and peer mentorship to create technology that makes people’s lives better.
